Discover the Birds of Monsoon

  1. Name: Red-vented Bulbul

    A familiar bird with a distinctive black crest and red under-tail coverts, often seen in gardens and forests.

  2. Name: Asian Koel

    Known for its melodious calls, this brood parasite is frequently heard during the monsoon.

  3. Name: Common Myna

    An adaptable bird with a brown body and yellow eye patches, commonly found in urban areas.

  4. Name: House Sparrow

    A small, social bird often seen around human habitation.

  5. Name: Spotted Dove

    Recognizable by the white-spotted black collar around its neck, commonly found in open areas.

  6. Name: Cattle Egret

    Often seen in fields and wetlands, these white birds are known to follow livestock.

  7. Name: Pied Bush Chat

    A small bird with contrasting black and white plumage, found in open fields and grasslands.

  8. Name: Oriental Magpie-Robin

    A small bird with a lovely song, found in gardens and forests.

  9. Name: Black Drongo

    A fearless bird often seen perched on wires and trees.

  10. Name: Blue-throated Barbet

    Recognized for its vibrant green and blue plumage.

  11. Name: White-throated Kingfisher

    A common sight near rivers and wetlands.

  12. Name: Greater Racket-tailed Drongo

    Noted for its long, rackety tail and mimicry skills.

  13. Name: Common Tailorbird

    Known for its unique nesting behavior, stitching leaves together to form a cradle for its eggs.

  14. Name: Scaly-breasted Munia

    A small, gregarious bird with a distinctive scaly pattern on its breast, often seen in flocks.

  15. Name: Asian Paradise Flycatcher

    A graceful bird with long tail feathers, especially males.
